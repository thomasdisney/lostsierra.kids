Mission & Philosophy

“At Lost Sierra Kids, we are dedicated to delivering exceptional, nature-inspired education to children in our rural community, nurturing curious minds and independent spirits through child-led exploration, hands-on learning, and respect for each child’s unique pace.”

Our days are filled with joyful discoveries in the forest, meadow, and classroom. We believe the natural world is the most inspiring teacher, and we build our curriculum around seasonal rhythms, play-based exploration, and nurturing relationships. Children grow through meaningful experiences—muddy boots, campfire songs, science experiments, handcrafts, and community connection.

We partner closely with families to create a home-away-from-home that celebrates each child’s unique voice. Respect, kindness, and a deep love for the Lost Sierra guide everything we do.

Curriculum at a Glance

Child-Led Learning

  • Outdoor adventures in the woods, meadows, and nearby creeks.
  • Project-based provocations inspired by the children’s interests.
  • Daily storytelling, music circles, and movement to build confidence.
  • Sensory exploration through gardening, mud kitchens, and nature art.

Creative Expression

  • Weekly art studios led by Stephanie with painting, weaving, and clay.
  • Science, cooking, and nature experiments that invite wonder.
  • Loose-parts play with pinecones, stones, fabric, and reclaimed treasures.
  • Seasonal celebrations honoring the rhythms of the Lost Sierra community.

Whole-Child Growth

  • Social-emotional learning through cooperative games and peace circles.
  • Emergent literacy and numeracy woven into play and daily routines.
  • Mindfulness moments, breathing, and rest to support regulation.
  • Nutritious, family-style meals and snacks to nourish growing bodies.

Meet Our Leadership

Stephanie

Administrative Director

Stephanie leads all day-to-day operations and on-site management. She coordinates classroom flow, mentors teachers and aides, and keeps families informed with warmth and clarity. In addition to managing scheduling, records, and conferences, Stephanie teaches weekly art, science, and kitchen experiments—infusing each lesson with creativity and curiosity.

Mahala

Creative Director

Mahala designs the curriculum and cultivates the educational vision of Lost Sierra Kids. She champions a nature-based, child-led philosophy by leading HR, training, and staff support. Mahala brings the magic to our branding, enrollment, and community partnerships, providing guidance while empowering Stephanie’s on-site leadership.

Shared Leadership in Action

Together, Stephanie and Mahala share equal responsibility for a thriving, joyful program. Mahala shapes the vision and structure; Stephanie brings it to life through daily classroom experiences. Their partnership balances creativity, organization, and deep community connection—ensuring every child and family feels seen, supported, and celebrated.

Rooted in Community

Lost Sierra Kids is woven into the fabric of Graeagle. We collaborate with local artisans, naturalists, and families to create experiences that celebrate our mountain home. Whether we’re gathering for a seasonal potluck, exploring nearby trails, or sharing produce from a neighbor’s garden, community connection is at the heart of our program.

We host parent workshops, nature stewardship days, and family campouts to strengthen relationships and support the whole child. Follow along on social media for event announcements and glimpses into our joyful days outdoors.
